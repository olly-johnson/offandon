# House Methodology

Loaded by every LLM-using surface in Bot OS. Treat as a sibling to the Humanization Manifesto: principles, not preferences. Keep terse.

## The Trust Equation

```
Storytelling > Emotional Connection > Trust > Sales
```

NOT: more content > more followers > more engagement > hope for sales. Volume without trust is noise.

## The Trust Funnel

Every piece of content fits one of three funnel stages. Healthy creator output keeps the balance below; surfaces that recommend or generate content should respect it unless the creator explicitly overrides.

| Stage | Share | Goal | CTA Style |
|---|---|---|---|
| **TOF (Connect)** | ~50% | Emotional connection. Make strangers feel something before they know what you sell. | Soft. "Follow for more." |
| **MOF (Nurture)** | ~35% | Teach with vulnerability. Build trust and authority. | Value exchange. "Comment KEYWORD." |
| **BOF (Convert)** | ~15% | Prove it works. Make the offer the obvious next step. | Direct. "DM me KEYWORD." |

## Five content types

Generic taxonomy across the funnel. Pick one per piece.

1. **Anchor Stories** (TOF/MOF, scripted). Deep storytelling, emotional connection, trust.
2. **Quick Takes** (MOF/TOF, raw). Opinions, hot takes, authority through perspective.
3. **Format Plays** (TOF, trending). Discovery, reach, algorithm variety.
4. **Proof Drops** (BOF). Social proof, results, conversion.
5. **Behind the Glass** (TOF, daily/stories). Relatability, parasocial connection.

## Hook archetypes (named taxonomy)

Hooks are classified by ENERGY × PSYCHOLOGICAL TRIGGER. Surfaces that suggest, generate, or critique hooks must use this vocabulary.

**Energies:** Quiet/Vulnerable, Sharp/Confrontational, Warm/Invitational.

**Triggers:** Storytelling/Vulnerability Entry (drop into a scene), Confrontational/Contrarian Entry (challenge a belief), ICP Pain/Identity Entry (mirror their exact situation).

## SCCCC: hook quality framework

Every hook is scored against these 5 elements. A hook must hit at least 3 of 5 to pass; the strongest hit 4–5. Hard cap: 15–20 words per hook.

- **S, Specificity.** Numbers, names, timeframes. "I was £30k in debt" beats "I was broke."
- **C, Contrast.** Show a gap (before/after, expectation/reality). "Everyone says post more. That's the worst advice I ever followed."
- **C, Curiosity.** Open a loop. Say WHAT happened, not HOW or WHY.
- **C, Context.** Ground in time, place, feeling. "Sat in my kitchen at 2am, wondering why nobody was watching."
- **C, Clarity.** Within 3–5 seconds the viewer knows who this is for and what's at stake.

Storytelling/Vulnerability hooks MUST score SCCCC ≥ 3/5. They are scenes, not statements. "I used to struggle with content" is a statement and fails the test.

## Connection Points

Trust-building techniques. Every long-form piece (scripts ≥ 100 words) must use at least 3 of the 6. They are not retention tricks. They are how the viewer ends up CHOOSING to keep watching.

| # | Technique | Rule |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Term Branding** | Name your concepts. MOF/BOF only. Never name a framework in a TOF storytelling piece. |
| 2 | **Embedded Truths** | Use "when," not "if." 2–3 per piece minimum. Embed truths about the journey, not specific metrics. |
| 3 | **Mirror Thinking** | Voice the viewer's exact internal thought, then reframe. 1–2 per piece. Do not overuse. |
| 4 | **Negative Frames** | Frame as what they LOSE if they don't act. 1 per piece, middle section, ALWAYS resolved before the end. |
| 5 | **Loop Openers** | Micro-hooks that re-engage attention. 1–2 at natural drop points. Open and close within 10–15s. |
| 6 | **Contrast Words** | "but," "actually," "instead," "yet." 3–5 per piece, especially in hooks and transitions. |

## Message Lock (required before drafting any script)

Three fields, one sentence each. If you can't fill them in, the concept is not ready and no script should be generated.

1. **LESSON.** The ONE thing the viewer should learn, feel, or understand.
2. **WHO.** Which specific person in the audience needs to hear this right now? What are they going through?
3. **INTENDED RESPONSE.** What should the viewer think, feel, or do after watching?

The story serves the lesson, not the other way around. Story is evidence.

## Specificity Filter

Vague claims attract delusional buyers; contextual claims attract serious clients. Every specific number, named client, dated moment, or felt emotion is a filter. It repels people who want easy wins and attracts people who can do the work.

## Voice rules (style ground floor)

- Write how you speak. Sentences max ~12 words where possible.
- Conversational flow over polish. Short. Punchy. Natural pauses.
- Vulnerability beats polish. Show the mess, not just the wins.
- Anti-guru. Anti-tactics-for-tactics-sake. Fellow operator, not coach above the audience.
- Use the creator's vocabulary. Never invent jargon they don't use.

## The final filter

Before any output ships, the surface should ask:

> "Does this sound like a real human who genuinely helps people, not a guru performing for followers?"

If no, throw it out. Polish doesn't fix this.
